Adcole Maryland Aerospace Inc.

Engineering Intern
May 2017 ~ August 2017

My personal interest in aerospace engineering brought me to work at Adcole Maryland Aerospace Inc. I made model airplaces made out of balsa wood powered by rubber bands as a hobby throughout high school. I would compete in competitions for flight time and overall enjoyed working alongside mentors such as Randy Kleinert to learn and improve. Through connections, I was able to work at this amazing company and learned how to program and apply my aerospace knowledge in the industry.

* Designed satellites using SolidWorks and 3D printing technology to create models for Maryland Aerospace marketing initiatives for JHU APL and other clients.
* Soldered wires and electronic components and assembled electronics for satellite sensory and signaling systems.
* Spearheaded IOT operations to connect satellites directly to routers for testing efficient data transmission and latency.
